# Folk of note — proposals (GM eyes only)

Candidates to join the named folk, for discussion before any of them enter
`data/characters.json`. Each is written to the same pattern as the seeded
five: a player-safe public description, a GM-only thread, and a building
they could one day hold as foreman. None of these are in the data yet.

---

## Mother Hale — midwife and setter of bones

**Public description.** Delivered half the settlement's children back in the
old country and remembers every birth-weight. Carries her whole surgery in
one basket. People tell her things while she works on them; she has never
once been heard repeating any of it.

**GM thread.** Her silence is professional and absolute — which means she
already holds more of the settlement's secrets than anyone, including some
the GM hasn't invented yet. A natural pressure valve: when a player secret
or a folk secret needs to *almost* surface, it surfaces in her tent first
and stops there. If she ever dies, the question of what she knew dies badly.

**Building.** An infirmary or healer's tent, when sickness or a hard winter
forces one. Aptitude sketch: Knowledge +2, Instinct +1.

---

## Tobin the Scrivener — a lettered man with one chest

**Public description.** Came across with a single sea-chest and let everyone
believe it was tools. It was books. Reads letters aloud for anyone who asks
and writes them for anyone who pays in eggs. Argues with himself in the
margins of things.

**GM thread.** The chest cost weight on the passage — somebody's second sack
of seed grain stayed on the dock so his books could come. He doesn't know
whose. One of the books is not like the others (this can stay vague until
the fiction earns it; rare-wonder rule §2 — if it's magical, it's enormous).

**Building.** The Library, when it unlocks — the event table already exists
(`library-event-table.json`). Aptitude sketch: Knowledge +3, Presence 0.

---

## Petra — the smith's widow

**Public description.** Her husband was to be the settlement's smith; the
fever took him a week out from shore. She keeps his tools oiled and wrapped
and will not let another hand touch them. Strong as a door and exactly as
easy to move.

**GM thread.** She can do the work. She watched him at the forge for twenty
years and her hands know it — but taking up the hammer means he is really
gone, so she hasn't. The settlement needs a forge before it needs her grief
resolved, which is the collision. Jory keeps cheerfully offering to build
her a smithy, which is a slow-motion catastrophe with a designed story
(§8C) standing right next to it.

**Building.** The Forge, when built. Aptitude sketch: Strength +2,
Finesse +2 — a *better* foreman than anyone expects, a mirror of Jory.

---

## Old Sef — the ferryman who won't touch the water

**Public description.** Poled a river ferry for forty years. Since the
crossing he will not go within a stone's throw of the shore, and nobody
asks why twice. Mends nets far from the tide line and is usually right
about tomorrow's weather.

**GM thread.** During the storm he saw something under the ship — and it
saw him back. He has been landlocked by that gaze ever since. This is a
rare-wonder thread: whatever it was is real, enormous, and patient, and
Sef is the only one who knows the settlement was *permitted* to arrive.

**Building.** A dock or fishery, one day — the cruel joke being that the
obvious foreman won't go near it. Aptitude sketch: Instinct +2, Agility +1.

---

*Format note: if adopted as folk of note, the public text goes in
`description` (players read it verbatim on `/table`) and the GM thread in
`hidden.notes`. Any of them could instead start as an unrevealed entry in
the People registry and be promoted to a folk card when they take a
building.*
