# The Bard's Systems — design blueprint (GM eyes only)

Oscar's 2026-07-17 session of ideas, gathered into one buildable design.
Everything here is **hidden layer** (spec §8): server-side, GM console
only, never in a player payload. The unifying fiction: the PCs and folk
were written into being by the Bard (or a trickster god) — the `/tome`
the players write in *is his book*. These systems are the machinery of
that authorship.

## 1. The reward pages (LLM prompter)

Each character earns **unique secret rewards** generated by an LLM
(Anthropic API; Oscar has credits) from a snapshot of *exactly who they
were on arrival* — the page the Bard wrote them on. If a player later
reinvents their character, the prepared rewards do not follow suit: the
Bard prepared a flying pony for who you were, even if you now want a
steed of death. That mismatch is a feature and a joke the table earns.

- **Input**: the creation-time PC snapshot (`data/pcs.json` entry as of
  `createdAt` — class, heritage, traits, experiences, description).
- **Output**: structured JSON, e.g. per PC: `[{ milestone, reward,
  presentation, whyThisCharacter }]` — validated against a schema, saved
  to `data/bard/rewards/<pcId>.json`, never regenerated once written
  (frozen pages). GM can hand-edit; it's JSON on disk like everything.
- **Trigger**: on character creation (or a GM "write their page" button
  during rollout for existing PCs). GM approval before anything is
  granted at the table.
- Suggested module: `server/bard.js` (no Express wiring inside; index.js
  mounts it). `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` from env; a `--dry-run` mode that
  prints the prompt without spending credits.

## 2. The audience (Suno likes → secret milestones)

Character themes live on Suno (Sol's music desk, `data/music.json`).
Their **real-world like-counts are the hidden applause of the Bard's
audience**: crossing thresholds triggers secret in-game rewards. Players
are literally being voted on by strangers and must never be told.

- Poll like-counts per linked song (Suno API or scrape — investigate;
  cache politely, e.g. daily).
- Milestone table in `data/bard/audience.json`: `{ songId → [{likes,
  reward, granted}] }`, GM-editable.
- Console nudge when a threshold crosses: *"The world has taken notice
  of Lyra."* GM decides how it lands in fiction.
- Extends to folk-of-note themes identically.

## 3. Silent portrait aging

Portraits are auto-generated and **forced** — because the pipeline needs
authority over faces. Every in-game year (a year passes when a new story
arc begins), a hand-crafted ComfyUI workflow ages every portrait via
img2img on the previous year's image.

- **Never overwrite**: archive per year — `data/portraits/<id>/year-<n>.png`.
- Year-advance flow: generate → **GM approval popup** per updated face →
  swap silently. No announcement anywhere.
- The journal/tome renders each entry's portrait **as-of that entry's
  year** — so players only notice when they look back and see how young
  they were. That look-back is the whole payoff; the archive is sacred.
- Folk age too (Garrick greying at the lumber camp).

## 4. Folk b-plot arcs (failure is the plot engine)

Each folk has a *natural path*: a **five-step b-plot arc** (pre-written
by the same Bard prompter from their card — the crossing-history threads
already in `hidden.notes` are exactly this material). Every year, one
hidden check per folk working at a building:

- **Success → stasis.** Life holds steady; no arc movement. A quiet year
  is a good year.
- **Failure → the arc advances one step**, and the GM receives one
  **scene brief**: basic scene information, nicely presented, enough to
  ad-hoc at the table. Injuries, deaths, and positive turns are arc
  steps, not separate tables.
- **Seasonal metronome**: briefs ride the season wheel — the first
  advance lands a scene in spring, then summer, autumn, winter — and the
  season colors the brief's tone (spring hopeful → winter grave, §2).
- **Modifiers**: inspiration (−1..+2) applies — cherished folk are
  mechanically shielded from tragedy, neglected folk drift toward their
  worst chapters, and nobody is told. **Age** applies as a stacking
  negative (needs an `age`/birth-year field on folk cards; the face and
  the odds weather together — "rip grannies").
- Storage: `data/bard/arcs/<charId>.json` — `{steps: [5], position,
  history: [{year, season, brief}]}`. Scene briefs render in the GM
  console only; they must never cross `views.js`.
- Die mechanics TBD with Oscar (bell-curve like everything else; never a
  flat roll).

## 5. Spoiler-safety constraints (bind all of it)

- All Bard data lives under `data/bard/` and is GM-only; no field of it
  may enter `tableView`, `loreView`, `screenView`, or any player payload.
- Rewards/arcs are *discovered through play, never announced* — same
  philosophy as inspiration and Jory's §8C arc.
- Generation happens against creation-time snapshots; the GM approves
  everything before it reaches the table.
- The tome may *quietly hint* at the metafiction (e.g. each character's
  first page already written, in a hand nobody claims, dated before the
  player arrived) — hints only, never exposition.

## Build order (suggested)

1. `server/bard.js` + reward-page generation for PCs (needs only the
   API key and a schema) — smallest end-to-end slice.
2. Folk `age` field + yearly check + arc storage (arc *generation* can
   start hand-written for the five seeded folk — their threads exist).
3. ComfyUI portrait wiring (the existing stub first, then the aging
   workflow once Oscar crafts it).
4. Suno polling + milestone nudges (depends on Sol's music desk data
   shape — coordinate via docs/worklog.md).
