# The Changed Knives

A short Tier 1 settlement-defense encounter for five level-1 PCs. This uses
reskinned Jagged Knife adversaries from the Daggerheart SRD revision dated
2025-09-09. The opening roster is deliberately lighter than a fully balanced
five-PC battle because this is the party's first combat and the session is
short.

Source: https://www.daggerheart.com/srd/

## Before the first roll

- Oore chooses the additional level-1 domain card owed by Prepared.
- Redneck and Heliga Erik roll their Prayer Dice.
- Ask each player where they are in the camp when the alarm is raised.
- Start with 2 Fear from the Ambushed environment, but use the enemy's first
  spotlight as a soft move against the supply wagon rather than immediate PC
  damage.

## Premise

At dusk, before the settlement has raised a single permanent building, figures
break from the tree line and descend on the wagon holding the shared food,
medicine, and tools. They wear a bandit's practical leathers, but their bodies
have been altered: joints knot in the wrong direction, old scars sprout black
glass, and dark sap moves visibly beneath the skin.

They are here to take the wagon, not to die. The mutation is recent, painful,
and not wholly under their control.

## Battlefield

- **Supply wagon:** Center of the camp. A creature on it has height and cover,
  but can be pulled down or trapped among the cargo.
- **Firepit:** Very Close to the wagon. Embers can expose a Hidden attacker,
  force movement, or start a dangerous camp fire on a roll with Fear.
- **Tents and guy lines:** Close around the wagon. A PC can use them to
  temporarily Restrain an adversary with an appropriate action roll (11).
- **Tree line:** Far from the wagon. The sniper begins Hidden here.
- **Settlers:** They are not combatants. Use them as threatened collateral and
  reasons to move, not as extra stat blocks.

Start a **Supplies Dragged Away (4)** Consequence Countdown. Tick it down when
an adversary at the wagon gets an uncontested spotlight, or when a PC rolls
with Fear while nobody is protecting it. At 0, the wagon reaches Far range and
the remaining fight becomes a chase. A successful action that decisively
secures the wagon can tick it up by 1, to a maximum of 4.

## Opening roster (10 Battle Points)

### Grafted Captain (Jagged Knife Lieutenant, Leader: 3)

Difficulty 13 | Thresholds 7/14 | HP 6 | Stress 3  
Attack +2 | Hooked Javelin, Close | 1d8+3 physical  
Experience: Pack Tactics +2

- **Shared Nerve (Action):** Mark a Stress when spotlighted to also spotlight
  two allies within Close range.
- **More Bodies (Action):** Call three Crooked Lackeys in at Far range. Save
  this for escalation.
- **Finish the Fallen (Action, Fear):** Attack a Vulnerable target within Close.
  On success, deal 2d6+12 physical damage and the target marks a Stress.
- **Feeding Momentum (Reaction):** Gain a Fear when the Captain succeeds on an
  attack against a PC.

Do not use Finish the Fallen in the first cycle. Telegraph it by having the
Captain notice and point toward a Vulnerable PC, giving the party time to act.

### Root-Hulk (Jagged Knife Kneebreaker, Bruiser: 4)

Difficulty 12 | Thresholds 7/14 | HP 7 | Stress 4  
Attack -3 | Knotted Club, Melee | 1d4+6 physical  
Experience: Unveiled Threats +3

- **Pinned Prey (Passive):** Creatures Restrained by the Hulk take double
  damage from other adversaries.
- **Long-Armed Hold (Action):** Attack a target in Melee. On success, deal no
  damage; the target becomes Restrained and Vulnerable. A successful Strength
  Roll frees them. They are also freed if the Hulk takes Major or greater
  damage.

The Hulk isolates somebody; the Captain makes the threat legible. Give the
party a spotlight before following through.

### Needle-Eye (Jagged Knife Sniper, Ranged: 2)

Difficulty 13 | Thresholds 4/7 | HP 3 | Stress 2  
Attack -1 | Shortbow, Far | 1d10+2 physical  
Experience: Stealth +2

- **Translucent Ambush (Passive):** A successful standard attack while Hidden
  deals 1d10+4 physical damage instead.

Needle-Eye repositions after being exposed and targets isolated characters,
not the same low-Evasion PC repeatedly.

### Five Crooked Lackeys (Minion group: 1)

Difficulty 9 | HP 1 each | Stress 1  
Attack -2 | Daggers, Melee | 2 physical  
Experience: Thief +2

- **Minion (3):** Any damage defeats one. For every 3 damage dealt, defeat one
  additional Lackey in range of the attack.
- **Dogpile (Action, Fear):** Spotlight all Lackeys within Close of one target.
  They move into Melee and make one shared attack; on success, combine 2
  physical damage per participating Lackey.

Their first priority is the wagon. Their second is helping the Hulk separate a
defender from the group.

## Optional escalation (+4 Battle Points)

If every PC has acted and the opening force is collapsing without creating a
real decision, bring in two Split-Skin Cutters from opposite sides of the camp.
Do not add them merely because the PCs are rolling well.

### Split-Skin Cutter (Jagged Knife Bandit, Standard: 2 each)

Difficulty 12 | Thresholds 8/14 | HP 5 | Stress 3  
Attack +1 | Daggers, Melee | 1d8+1 physical  
Experience: Thief +2

- **Jointed Climber (Passive):** Climbs as easily as running.
- **Drop Attack (Passive):** A successful standard attack from above deals
  1d10+1 physical damage instead.

## Running the fight

1. The Captain's first move points the lackeys at the wagon and tells the PCs
   what will be lost. No damage yet.
2. Move the spotlight through players who have not acted. A failure usually
   gives the GM a move; a Fear result supplies pressure or collateral.
3. Use the Hulk to create a rescue problem, the Sniper to make the tree line
   matter, and the Captain to coordinate. Avoid focus-firing one PC.
4. Use the optional Cutters only if the players need another combat cycle.
5. When the Captain and Hulk are defeated, or when half the force is gone and
   escape is possible, the survivors flee or surrender. Do not grind through
   every remaining HP.

## Evidence and aftermath

Keep the cause open tonight. The characters can establish these facts:

- The attackers were ordinary bandits until recently.
- The changes grew through old wounds first.
- The black sap smells of wet iron and reacts faintly to nearby magic.
- The Captain carries a rough charcoal sketch of the new camp. Someone knew
  where the settlers had stopped.

A living captive can be questioned later. The immediate reward is that the
settlement keeps its first shared supplies and learns that the wilderness has
already noticed them.
